Faculty Advisors

Renowned Statistics Professors at the UC Berkeley Statistics Deparment act as faculty advisors to support and give our officers advice.

David Aldous

Evans 351

Professor David Aldous received his Ph.D. from Cambridge University and has worked at Berkeley since 1979. His research in probability has covered Markov chains, random networks, and stochastic coalescence. In addition, he teaches several upper division statistics courses and is a Fellow of the Royal Society and a foreign associate of the National Academy of Sciences.

Adityanand Guntuboyina

Evans 423

Assistant Professor Adityanand Guntuboyina received his Ph.D in statistics and has worked at Berkeley since 2012 after finishing his post-doc at the University of Pennsylvania. He is currently working on statistical problems involving convexity typfied by the estimation of convex sets and functions from various types of noisy measurements.
